Facundo Lema

I am a 20-year-old mathematics and physics student at University of Buenos Aires. I am interested in computer graphics, topology, geometry and CGI. I love computers, numbers and science.

On my last year of high school (2019), I started planning a trip across Europe, a journey that began at the very beginning of 2020. During the trip, I decided to move to Sweden for a year to learn the language. I have always found Scandinavia a very interesting place, and I regard Sweden as one of the most beautiful countries in Europe.

After a short period living in Sweden, the COVID‑19 outbreak reached the country and I suddenly had lots of time to spend indoors. I sought to improve myself in different aspects. On one hand, I made significant improvements in Swedish skills, and on the other, I started learning more about things I had always found interesting but never had the time to study about: Design, CGI and Computer Science stood out.

I started attending Harvard's CS50x: Introduction to Computer Science lectures through EdX, learning a ton of new things every class and using that acquired knowledge later in every problem set. Simultaneously, I learned how to use Blender, an awesome free, open‑source 3D software, along with many other apps from Adobe Creative Cloud, such as Photoshop, Lightroom, Illustrator, InDesign, Premiere Pro and After Effects. I also bought an iPad and started doing illustrations on Procreate.

All that interaction with design software, combined with everything I was learning about programming and the inner works of a computer forced me to look into the mathematics and physics behind them. I began asking questions: How accurate are Blender's physics simulations? How can a coumputer accurately represent 3D space? How does 3D rendering work? Maths behind ray‑tracing? Millions of calculations per second for each frame in a game?!

All those questions, and their answers, made me want to study mathematics and physics. After a year in Sweden, I returned to Argentina and started studying physics at University of Buenos Aires. I attended Harvard's CS50's Understanding Technology through its EdX course. I also started building computers, first one for myself, then for some family members, friends and at the end I even sold some. Additionally, I built a few bespoke mining rigs for some clients.

To come to the point, I love computers (both hardware and software), programming, mathematics, physics, design, art, computer graphics, algorithms and more, and I wish to keep learning, as much as possible, in every possible occasion.
